# Considerations for Applying for a Recreational Freshwater Fishing Licence in British Columbia

This document summarizes the key considerations for anyone applying for a recreational freshwater (non-tidal) fishing licence in British Columbia, based on information from the BC Government's official fishing and hunting pages.

---

## 1. Who Needs a Licence

- A valid freshwater fishing licence is required to fish for **any species in non-tidal (fresh) waters** in BC.
- **BC residents aged 16 and older** require a basic licence.
- **Anglers under 16** may fish without a licence and are not subject to licence quotas.
- **First Nations individuals** recognized under the Indian Act do not require a licence.
- **Non-residents** (Canadians from outside BC) and **non-resident aliens** (non-Canadians) aged 16+ also require a licence, at higher fee rates than residents.

---

## 2. Licence Types and Fees

### BC Residents
| Licence Type | Fee |
|---|---|
| Annual | $41.15 |
| Eight-day | $22.86 |
| One-day | $11.43 |
| Annual (Seniors 65+) | $5.71 |
| Reduced-fee program (severe/permanent disability) | $1.14 |

### Non-Residents / Non-Resident Aliens
| Licence Type | Non-Resident | Alien |
|---|---|---|
| Annual | $62.87 | $91.44 |
| Eight-day | $41.15 | $57.14 |
| One-day | $22.86 | $22.86 |

### Validity
- Annual licences run from **April 1 to March 31** each year.
- Licences are **non-refundable and non-transferable**.

---

## 3. How to Apply / Purchase

- Licences can be purchased **online through the WILD system** or **in person at licensed retail vendors**.
- A **Fish and Wildlife ID (WID)** is required as part of the application/purchase process.
- Once purchased, anglers can **log in to WILD** to view, download, or reprint their licence at any time.
- Anglers must **carry their Fish and Wildlife ID and photo ID** while fishing.
- Revenue from licence sales supports fish stocking and conservation programs.

---

## 4. Angling Licence Fee Reduction Program (for Persons with Disabilities)

For BC residents with a **severe and permanent disability**, the reduced annual licence fee of **$1.14** is available (instead of the regular $41.15).

**Important notes:**
- The reduction applies **only to the basic annual licence**. It does **not** apply to:
  - Conservation Surcharge Stamps
  - White Sturgeon Conservation Licence
  - Classified Waters Licence

**Application process:**
1. Complete the official application form.
2. Provide documentation confirming receipt of specific disability-related benefits (listed in **Part B** of the form).
3. If such documentation is unavailable, a **practicing registered BC physician or nurse** must complete **Part C** instead.

**Submission:**
- Deliver in person at most **Service BC** locations, or
- Mail to the **Wildlife Branch in Victoria** (processing takes ~10 days for mailed applications).

**After approval:**
- Applicants receive a **Certificate of Eligibility**, required when purchasing the discounted licence from a vendor.
- Those who previously qualified but lost documentation can confirm eligibility through **FrontCounter BC**.

---

## 5. Classified Waters Licences

Some of BC's most productive trout streams are designated as **Classified Waters** (Class I or Class II) — there are **52 such waters** province-wide. Fishing these waters requires an additional licence on top of the basic licence.

### Fees
- **Residents:** $17.15 annually (covers both Class I and Class II waters)
- **Non-residents:** 
  - Class I: $45.72/day
  - Class II: $22.86/day
  - Non-residents **cannot purchase an annual** Classified Waters Licence

### How to Obtain
- Available at any vendor that sells basic fishing licences.
- **Pre-sales open March 1 each year**, allocated **first-come, first-served** through WILD.

### Quotas / Restrictions
- The **Kootenay region** uses a booking system with bi-weekly angler-day allocations for non-guided, non-resident anglers on three rivers: **Michel Creek, Skookumchuck Creek, and Wigwam River**.
- **Maximum 8 consecutive days** per non-resident licence.
- The **Dean River** has special restrictions — non-resident aliens are limited to **one licence per year** (see Section 7 below).

---

## 6. Conservation Surcharge Stamps

A basic licence can be validated with up to **five annual Conservation Surcharge stamps**, plus a separate **White Sturgeon Conservation Licence**, depending on the species being targeted/retained.

- Stamps are valid for the **licence year (April 1 – March 31)**, starting on the date of purchase.
- Anglers must **immediately record retained fish** on their licence.

### When Stamps Are Required
| Situation | Stamp Required |
|---|---|
| Steelhead (anywhere in BC, retain or release) | Yes — limited to 1 stamp/year; province-wide hatchery quota of 10 fish |
| Non-tidal salmon (to retain, except kokanee) | Yes — not required for catch-and-release |
| Kootenay Lake rainbow trout > 50 cm | Yes |
| Shuswap Lake rainbow trout > 50 cm | Yes |
| Shuswap Lake char > 60 cm | Yes |
| White Sturgeon (Fraser River Watershed, catch-and-release only) | Separate White Sturgeon Conservation Licence required |

### Costs (per 2026 pricing, taxes excluded)
| Stamp Type | Resident | Non-Resident |
|---|---|---|
| Steelhead | $28.57 | $68.57 |
| Non-tidal salmon | $17.14 | $34.29 |
| Rainbow/char stamps | $11.43 | $22.86 |
| White Sturgeon (annual) | $28.57 | $68.58 |

## 7. Dean River Draw (Non-Resident Aliens Only)

The **Dean River draw** is an annual lottery allowing **non-resident alien (non-Canadian)** anglers to compete for licences to fish the **Class I – Main Section of the Dean River without a guide**.

### Eligibility
- Open only to **non-resident alien anglers** — applying individually or as a group.
- Anglers **under 16** may apply but must be accompanied by a licensed adult while fishing.

### How to Apply
1. Register for a **Fish and Wildlife ID (WID)** if not already held.
2. Select preferred fishing dates between **June 21 and September 30** (up to 3 choices, each up to 8 consecutive days).
3. Submit the application form — **no application fee**.
4. **Group applications** must list identical date selections for all members and be submitted as a single application.

### Deadlines
- The official page noted the draw is **currently closed for applications**; check the BC government site for updated annual deadlines.

### Licences Required After Winning the Draw
Successful applicants aged 16+ must obtain:
1. **Basic freshwater fishing licence**
2. **Steelhead Conservation Surcharge Stamp**
3. **Dean Class I Upper – Draw Classified Waters Licence**

### Note for Canadian Residents
Canadian residents (BC residents and non-resident Canadians) are **not subject to the draw** and may purchase Classified Waters Licences for the Dean River directly, subject to the standard once-per-year restriction noted in Section 5.

## 8. Summary Checklist for Applicants

- [ ] Confirm your residency category (BC resident, non-resident Canadian, or non-resident alien) — this determines fees and eligibility for certain programs.
- [ ] Register for a **Fish and Wildlife ID (WID)** if you don't already have one.
- [ ] Choose your licence duration (one-day, eight-day, or annual) based on how often you plan to fish.
- [ ] Determine whether you qualify for a **reduced fee** (seniors 65+, or the disability fee reduction program — apply for the Certificate of Eligibility in advance if applicable).
- [ ] Check whether your intended fishing locations are **Classified Waters** — if so, purchase the appropriate Classified Waters Licence (note pre-sale date of March 1 and quota/booking systems in some regions).
- [ ] Determine whether your target species (steelhead, non-tidal salmon, large rainbow trout/char in Kootenay or Shuswap Lakes, or White Sturgeon) require a **Conservation Surcharge Stamp** or separate conservation licence.
- [ ] If you are a **non-resident alien** wanting to fish the Dean River, check the draw status and application window (dates between June 21 – September 30).
- [ ] Purchase your licence(s) through **WILD** (online) or an authorized retail vendor, and keep your **Fish and Wildlife ID + photo ID** with you while fishing.
- [ ] Remember licences are **non-refundable and non-transferable**, and annual licences expire **March 31**.
- [ ] Consult the current year's **Freshwater Fishing Regulations Synopsis** for region-specific rules, catch limits, and exceptions.
